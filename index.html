the modules portfolio and digital portfolio

Module is organised by LUCA School of Arts, Visual Arts, Gent and part of the course Professionalisation.



Course subjects

The course handles theoretical (digital archives, image editing, copyright, social media, ...) and practical aspects of online communication in art practices.

The coursematerial (frameworks and repositories) we use for the practical side of this course are published on Github.

Online communication

The content of online publications are structured with a content management system (CMS), an application that allows to edit archived content into articles.

Frontend Frameworks

The content of online publications is published with frontend frameworks. That contains HTML and CSS-based design templates for typography, buttons, navigation and other interface components.

During the portfoliocourse we use Bureaupixel Foundation Theme (based on Foundation) & Bureaupixel Bootstrap Theme (based on Bootstrap).

OPENSOURCE SOFTWARE

Shared code or repositories are the basis of the coursemethod we use in this module. Students get aquianted with several systems of opensource code and software, used in art practices.

COPYRIGHT, PRIVACY EN STATISTICS

What is the impact of digital publications on copyright? What information does google-analytics provide and how does that influence my online publication?

ONLINE COMMUNICATION, IMAGE-EDITING AND -COMPRESSION

Collecting data in a digital archive, metadata and image-editing, online communication in art practices.

MULTIMEDIA IN ART PRACTICES

Another starting point for this module is the diverse character of contemporary art practices. An essay "de hybride kunstenaar" of Camiel van Winkel, Pascal Gielen & Koos Zwaan (in dutch) provides ome interesting data on this subject.

Second Bachelor / 2nd phase

Students from the second bachelor focus on the editorial aspect of a digital publication. Archive and prepare data for publication, in close collaboration with the module on Studio- & reproduction-photography. This results in a webpublication of one page.

Third Bachelor / 3th phase

Students of the third bachelor setup a fully documented portfolio; they start of from the data collected in the previous phases, add and select content, structure it into a digital publication of multiple pages, that fully documents their artistic process.
